Digital Arts Circus

The new-media bohemia gathers in Belgrade

{% trans %}

New-media art rarely gets sold at Christie's. It's made by outsiders to the world of fine art whose mode of artistic expression is computer code.

The new-media bohemia opens up its temporary ateliers where there is electricity and material to tinker with. Some contribute remotely. Every bit of creative input, every snippet of code becomes part of the collective history of the artifact.

Not many of them are at ease calling themselves artists. Their self-descriptions would provide enough material for several business cards. If they have any. They teach at universities, work for clients, newspapers or technology companies.

José Luis de Vicente, a cultural researcher and curator based in Barcelona, calls new-media artists the "unpaid research & development departments of the advertising industry". We have met them at Resonate festival in Belgrade.
